Risotto Alla Millanese

Description

Risotto Alla Milanese is a special dish from Italian cuisine that originates from Milan. It is a delicate and creamy risotto that gets its characteristic golden color from saffron. Preparing Risotto Alla Milanese requires patience and precision. The rice is sautéed in butter or olive oil with finely chopped onions, and then gradually simmered with broth until it becomes tender and creamy. Additionally, white dry wine and Parmesan cheese are added to the risotto, giving it a rich flavor. Risotto Alla Milanese is a perfect accompaniment to various meat dishes or can be enjoyed on its own with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh parsley. This exceptional risotto delights not only with its taste but also with its beautiful appearance, making it a true delight for the palate.

Ingredients

  • 1,2l of water
  • 3/4 teaspoon of fine sea salt
  • 4 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1/2 finely chopped white onion
  • 1 cup (200g) of Carnaroli or Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup (120ml) of dry white wine
  • 2/3 cup (60g) of grated Parmesan cheese
  • 3 tablespoons of mascarpone
  • 1/2 teaspoon of grated lemon zest
  • freshly ground pepper

What to do

Bring water to a boil with 1 teaspoon of salt. Reduce the heat to low. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or oven-proof pot over medium heat. Sauté the onion with a pinch of salt for 4-5 minutes until golden brown. Add the rice and stir well to coat it with the oil. Sauté, stirring frequently, for 3-4 minutes until the rice becomes translucent. Increase the heat, pour in the wine, and let it evaporate. Pour in the first ladle of prepared water, about 1/3 cup. Stir and cook for 2-3 minutes until the rice absorbs almost all of the water. Stir, add the next portion of water. Repeat the process until the rice becomes plump, cooked, but still al dente. You may not need to use all the water, but the mixture should be liquid (more than your intuition suggests). This will take around 20-25 minutes. Remove from heat. Stir in the Parmesan cheese, then add the mascarpone and lemon zest. The risotto should be very creamy and liquid - it should spread on the plate. Add more water if needed. Season with freshly ground pepper. Serve it as is, with just a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese and lemon zest. Or with your favorite additions. Anchovies, sautéed zucchini slices, and a pinch of cumin work great. It's umami, light, and slightly exotic.
