Pasta Al Limone

Description

The lightest of all pastas. A significant portion of its sauce is... water. Yes, you read that right, water from the cooking pasta plays a crucial role here, and although there's also cream and butter, it only strengthens our belief that it will be truly divine. Most importantly, the main star is lemon (used from every possible angle), which transforms the pasta into something truly extraordinary. But, you have to try it for yourselves.

Ingredients

  • 1 lemon
  • 200 g pasta (preferably spaghetti or tagliatelle)
  • ½ cup (120 ml) heavy cream (30% fat)
  • 3 full tablespoons cold butter
  • ⅓ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • freshly ground pepper, salt

What to do

Use a vegetable peeler or a knife to remove a 5 cm strip of lemon zest. Finely chop it into thin strips. Grate the remaining lemon zest using a fine grater, measure out half of it, and save the rest for other culinary purposes. Squeeze 1 tablespoon of lemon juice and set it aside. In a medium saucepan, pour the heavy cream and add the lemon zest. Bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and, while continuously stirring with a whisk, add the cold butter one tablespoon at a time. Make sure each piece is melted before adding the next. Meanwhile, cook the pasta al dente (1 minute less than the time indicated on the package, as it will continue cooking in the sauce). Measure out 1 cup of the pasta cooking water and add ⅓ cup of it to the creamy butter sauce along with the pasta. The sauce may seem too liquid and resemble soup, but it will thicken significantly. Increase the heat to medium and add the grated Parmesan cheese, one tablespoon at a time, while stirring frequently. Season with salt, pepper, and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice. If needed, thin out the sauce with more of the pasta cooking water. Transfer the pasta to serving bowls, sprinkle with the previously chopped lemon zest, and add an extra amount of freshly ground pepper.
